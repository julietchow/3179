Evaluating Energy Efficiency

In our analysis, we employed a scatter plot that charts energy production versus energy consumption per capita, and we integrated a continent drop-down list feature for a comprehensive exploration.

To interpret the scatter plot effectively, we considered the position of data points concerning the diagonal line. If a data point appears above this line, it signifies a country that efficiently produces more energy than it consumes, reflecting a sustainable energy model.

Moreover, most of leading global energy consumers show a remarkable balance between energy consumption and production, reflecting a strong energy management approach. Notably, Angola emerges as a remarkable outlier, demonstrating an excess in energy production compared to consumption, indicating untapped energy potential or significant energy exports.

Yet, some nations, like Singapore and Hong Kong, grapple with energy deficits, relying on imports due to consumption exceeding production. These insights from our scatter plot provide a valuable lens to evaluate countries' energy efficiency, sustainability, and their positions within the global energy landscape.
