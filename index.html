Global Energy Consumption 2000-2021

Energy consumption is measured in sextillion kilowatt-hours (kWh)

Two Columns Layout

Energy consumption refers to the measurement of how much energy is used or expended within a specific period. In the context of the visualization, it represents the total energy usage worldwide from the years 2000 to 2021. This visualization is presented as a world map, allowing users to explore and understand global energy consumption patterns during this timeframe.

Users can interact with the map using a dropdown menu that lets them select from different energy sources, including coal, petroleum, natural gas, and nuclear & renewables. By choosing a specific energy source, users can gain insights into how these sources have contributed to the world's energy needs over the years.

To provide a dynamic and time-based analysis, a year slider is incorporated, enabling users to select a particular year within the 2000-2021 range. This feature allows for a detailed examination of how energy consumption and source distribution have evolved over the past two decades.

In essence, this interactive visualization serves as a powerful tool for comprehending the ever-changing landscape of global energy dynamics. It offers valuable insights into how different energy sources have been utilized over time and can help in making informed decisions related to energy policy, sustainability, and environmental concerns.
